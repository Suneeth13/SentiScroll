### 1. Real-World Problem Description: Fragmented Search and Semantic Friction in Cross-Platform E-Commerce

The Indian e-commerce landscape is highly fragmented, with millions of consumers navigating a multitude of platforms such as Flipkart, Myntra, Amazon, and JioMart daily. A critical barrier to efficient shopping is "Semantic Friction": traditional search architectures on these platforms rely on keyword-matching rather than natural language understanding. Consumers struggle to execute complex, multi-attribute queries—such as "1400 RPM non-BLDC ceiling fans under ₹2000"—often forced to manually toggle dozens of brittle filters or settle for irrelevant results.

While ecosystem-locked assistants like Amazon’s 'Rufus' have introduced conversational shopping, they are restricted to a single "walled garden." There is a total absence of a universal, platform-neutral tool that can assist a consumer across the entire web. This results in "Tab Fatigue," where users must constantly switch between tabs, copy-paste specifications, and manually compare unstructured product data across competing retailers, leading to decision paralysis and suboptimal purchases.

### 2. Problem Statement: Universal Agentic Interface for Localized E-Commerce

The objective of this project is to develop a browser-based, AI-native shopping copilot that provides a unified conversational layer over any e-commerce website. The system aims to democratize "Agentic Commerce" by allowing users to interact with any shopping portal via a persistent, context-aware sidebar. By reasoning over a website’s real-time Document Object Model (DOM), the assistant interprets complex natural language requirements, filters through unstructured product lists, and presents the consumer with a curated "Semantic View" of the best matches, significantly reducing the cognitive load of high-intent shopping.

### 3. Research Gap: Semantic DOM Compression and Platform-Agnostic Commerce

Despite the rapid advancement of LLMs, most current research is focused on either closed-loop marketplace assistants (like Rufus) or external search engines (like Perplexity) that provide static links. There is a significant research gap in **"Real-time Semantic DOM Compression"**—the ability for a lightweight AI agent to dynamically parse and reason over the heavily unstructured, JS-rendered HTML of varied retailer sites in real-time. Currently, no generalized framework exists that can successfully navigate the "DOM Drift" (layout changes) across different retailers to render an interactive, on-page product discovery layer that is platform-neutral.

### 4. Technical Limitations & Shortfalls of Existing Solutions

Existing shopping tools fall short in four key dimensions:
1. **Ecosystem Locking (The Rufus Problem):** State-of-the-art conversational shopping is currently trapped within single ecosystems, providing no value to consumers shopping on Indian-specific platforms like Flipkart or local niche stores.
2. **Brittle Heuristics:** Current browser extensions (like Honey or Capital One) rely on hardcoded CSS selectors and heuristics for coupons and price-tracking. They cannot perform "Deep Reasoning" over product specifications or handle "Out-of-Distribution" layout changes.
3. **Passive Link-Sharing:** External AI tools (ChatGPT, etc.) generate text-based suggestions or links but cannot interact with the user’s current active session or render "Native-Style" interactive product cards directly on the page.
4. **Contextual Blindness:** Most search tools ignore the user’s live browsing context. They cannot "see" what the user is currently looking at to provide comparative insights ("The fan you are looking at is 1200RPM, but this other one on the page is 1400RPM for the same price").
5. **DOM Processing Costs:** Naive LLM-based scrapers are too slow and context-heavy to run in a browser environment. Standard solutions fail to compress complex, token-heavy HTML into a structured format that allows for low-latency, "on-page" interactive dialogue.

This project addresses a problem of sufficient technical depth, requiring a fusion of Browser-Automation, Multimodal GenAI, and real-time DOM-Reasoning—exactly the high-impact innovation targeted by XploreInnoQuest.
